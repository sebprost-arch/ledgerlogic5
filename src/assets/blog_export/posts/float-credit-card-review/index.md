## Frequently Asked Questions


This section covers key topics about how the Float credit card works, requirements, fees, activation processes, and alternatives.
### How does the Float credit card operate?


The Float credit card operates on a prepaid model. Businesses fund the card through their bank accounts. It functions like a credit card but draws from the preloaded funds. This mechanism ensures controlled spending and helps manage business expenses efficiently.
### Are there any credit checks required for the Float card?


No, Float does not require a credit check for approval. Since the card is prepaid and tied to your business bank account, your credit history is not a factor in the application process.
### What are the associated fees with using a Float card?


Float offers a free basic plan with no monthly fees. They also offer a premium plan costing $99 per month, which includes advanced features. There are no foreign transaction fees, but other charges could apply depending on the usage.
### What is the annual percentage rate (APR) on balances with Float card?


Float cards do not have an APR because they are prepaid and do not carry a balance like traditional credit cards. Funds are preloaded, so cardholders spend only what is available.
### How can I activate my Float card?


To activate your Float card, submit a quick application and connect your business bank account. Once funds are added, the card is ready for use. Activation typically completes within one business day after submitting your application.
### What are some alternatives to the Float card currently available?


Some alternatives to the Float card include the Caary Business Mastercard and traditional corporate credit cards. These options offer various features, fees, and rewards programs that might suit different business needs.
